## Data Protection & Privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long does XtendOps retain my data?">
    XtendOps retains data according to specific retention periods based on data type and legal requirements:

    - **Customer Data**: 3 years after last purchase
    - **Cardholder Data**: 2 weeks (based on backup and recovery strategy)
    - **Audit Logs**: 3 years
    - **Contractual Records**: 5 years after contract end

    Data is not held longer than required for business or legal purposes. See our [Security & Compliance](/security/general) page for the complete retention schedule.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data when it's no longer needed?">
    When data reaches the end of its retention period, XtendOps follows a secure destruction process:

    1. **Secure Destruction**: Data is destroyed in a manner that ensures it cannot be recovered
    2. **Certified Disposal**: Industry-certified disposal companies handle sensitive data destruction
    3. **Documentation**: A certificate of safe disposal is obtained and retained for audit purposes

    This ensures your data is permanently and securely removed from our systems.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I request deletion of my data before the retention period ends?">
    Yes, you can request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal and contractual obligations. Contact your XtendOps account representative or our security team at security@xtendops.com to initiate a data deletion request.

    Note that some data may need to be retained to comply with legal, regulatory, or contractual requirements (e.g., financial records for tax purposes).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is my data protected during storage?">
    XtendOps implements multiple layers of protection:

    - **Encryption**: Data is encrypted using industry-standard cryptographic techniques
    - **Access Control**: Strict access controls limit who can view or modify data
    - **Secure Storage**: Electronic data is stored on secure, encrypted systems
    - **Environmental Controls**: Physical records are stored in controlled-access facilities
    - **Backup & Recovery**: Regular backups ensure data availability and disaster recovery
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where is my data stored?">
    XtendOps stores data in secure facilities with:

    - **Primary Storage**: Encrypted electronic systems with strict access controls
    - **Backup Storage**: Offsite backups for disaster recovery purposes
    - **Physical Storage**: Controlled-access facilities with environmental controls for any paper records

    All storage locations comply with relevant data protection and security regulations.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Payment Card Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is XtendOps PCI DSS compliant?">
    Yes, XtendOps is fully compliant with the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS). We undergo regular audits and maintain strict controls to protect cardholder data.

    Our PCI DSS compliance ensures:
    - Secure handling of payment card information
    - Protection against data breaches
    - Regular security assessments and updates
    - Compliance with card brand requirements
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does XtendOps store my credit card CVV/CVC code?">
    **No.** XtendOps does not store Sensitive Authentication Data (SAD), even in encrypted form. This includes:

    - CVV/CVC/CVV2/CID security codes
    - Full magnetic stripe data
    - PIN numbers or PIN blocks

    This data is rendered unrecoverable immediately after the authorization stage of the payment process. This is a strict PCI DSS requirement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are credit card numbers protected?">
    When Primary Account Numbers (PANs) must be stored for legitimate business purposes, XtendOps implements strict security controls:

    **Masking**: PANs are masked when displayed, showing only the first 6 and last 4 digits

    **Encryption**: Full PANs are encrypted using industry-standard cryptography

    **Access Control**: Only authorized personnel with legitimate business needs can access full PANs:
    - Operations Director (verification purposes)
    - Chief Information Security Officer (security oversight)
    - ICT Team (technical protection and control)

    **Transmission Security**: PANs are never sent via email, IM, SMS, or webchat
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What cardholder data does XtendOps retain?">
    XtendOps only retains cardholder data necessary for legitimate business purposes, and only for 2 weeks based on our backup and recovery strategy.

    **We DO NOT store:**
    - CVV/CVC codes
    - Full magnetic stripe data
    - PIN numbers

    **We MAY store (encrypted):**
    - Primary Account Number (PAN) - masked in displays
    - Cardholder name
    - Expiration date
    - Transaction details
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Compliance & Regulations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What compliance standards does XtendOps follow?">
    XtendOps maintains compliance with multiple frameworks:

    - **PCI DSS**: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard
    - **SOX**: Sarbanes-Oxley Act for financial data (7-year retention)
    - **Data Protection Laws**: Applicable privacy and data protection regulations
    - **Employment Law**: HR data retention requirements

    We conduct regular reviews to ensure continued compliance with all applicable standards.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How often are security policies reviewed?">
    XtendOps conducts regular reviews of data retention and security practices. Our Data Retention and Protection Policy is reviewed at least annually.

    Reviews ensure:
    - Policies remain valid and current
    - Data is retained according to established policies
    - Legal and regulatory requirements are fulfilled
    - Security controls remain effective
    - PCI DSS compliance is maintained

    All review results are documented for audit purposes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does XtendOps comply with GDPR/data protection laws?">
    Yes, XtendOps complies with applicable data protection and privacy regulations. Our data retention policies align with data protection requirements, including:

    - Lawful basis for data processing
    - Data minimization principles
    - Storage limitation (data not held longer than necessary)
    - Security and confidentiality
    - Individual rights (access, deletion, portability)

    Contact our security team for specific data protection inquiries.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data Access & Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who has access to my data?">
    XtendOps follows the principle of least privilege - access is granted only to personnel with legitimate business needs.

    **Access Control Measures:**
    - Role-based access controls
    - Regular access reviews
    - Audit logging of all access
    - Multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems
    - Background checks for personnel with data access

    All access is monitored and logged for security purposes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are encryption keys managed?">
    XtendOps implements secure key management practices:

    - **Secure Storage**: Encryption keys are stored securely for the life of the data
    - **Access Control**: Keys are protected with the same or higher security classification as the data they protect
    - **Policy Compliance**: Key management follows our Cryptographic Policy
    - **Regular Rotation**: Keys are rotated according to security best practices
    - **Separation of Duties**: Key management responsibilities are segregated
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if there's a security incident?">
    XtendOps maintains incident response procedures to quickly address security concerns:

    1. **Detection**: Continuous monitoring identifies potential incidents
    2. **Response**: Immediate action to contain and remediate
    3. **Investigation**: Forensic analysis using retained audit logs (3-year retention)
    4. **Notification**: Affected parties notified as required by law
    5. **Remediation**: Root cause analysis and preventive measures

    To report a security concern, contact security@xtendops.com immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I verify XtendOps security practices?">
    XtendOps maintains transparency about our security practices:

    - **Security Documentation**: Available to customers upon request
    - **Compliance Certifications**: PCI DSS and other certifications
    - **Security Questionnaires**: We respond to customer security assessments
    - **Audit Reports**: Available under NDA for enterprise customers

    Contact your account representative for specific security documentation requests.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Technical Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What encryption standards does XtendOps use?">
    XtendOps uses industry-standard encryption algorithms for data protection:

    - **Data at Rest**: Strong encryption for stored data
    - **Data in Transit**: TLS/SSL for all data transmission
    - **Algorithm Standards**: Industry-recognized encryption standards
    - **Regular Updates**: Cryptographic methods updated to maintain effectiveness
    - **PCI DSS Compliance**: Encryption meets or exceeds PCI DSS requirements

    See our Cryptographic Policy for detailed technical specifications.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long are audit logs retained?">
    Security audit logs (logon/logoff events, permission changes, etc.) are retained for **3 years**.

    This retention period allows for:
    - Forensic investigations of security incidents
    - Compliance auditing and reporting
    - Pattern analysis for security improvements
    - Legal and regulatory requirements

    Audit logs are stored securely with integrity protection to prevent tampering.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can data be retrieved after it's deleted?">
    No. When data is securely destroyed at the end of its retention period, it cannot be recovered.

    XtendOps uses:
    - **Secure Deletion Methods**: Data is overwritten or cryptographically erased
    - **Certified Disposal**: Industry-certified companies handle physical media destruction
    - **Verification**: Destruction is verified and documented

    This ensures permanent removal of data from all systems and backups.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does XtendOps handle backups?">
    XtendOps maintains robust backup and recovery procedures:

    - **Regular Backups**: Automated backup schedules for all critical data
    - **Encrypted Storage**: Backups are encrypted and stored securely
    - **Offsite Storage**: Disaster recovery backups stored at separate locations
    - **Retention Alignment**: Backups follow the same retention policies as primary data
    - **Recovery Testing**: Regular testing ensures backup integrity and recoverability

    Cardholder data backups are retained for a maximum of 2 weeks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
